Mid And South Essex NHS Foundation Trust

After nearly a decade of Conservative rule, on average, 292 more people are being left to wait over four hours at A&E at your hospital than back in 2010.

And things are bad for the rest of England too. Each month over 400,000 more people are made to wait over four hours to be seen at A&E than in 2011.

Now, Brexit and an impending trade deal with Trump's US threatens to increase the NHS drug bill from £18 billion to as much as £45 billion a year while shutting out the nurses, carers and other workers that the health service depends on.

Note: Over the past few months A&E attendance across England has fallen by more than 50% as people choose to stay at home to help reduce the pressure on the NHS during the current pandemic.

Call to Action

For better or for worse, the number of beds owned by Mid And South Essex NHS Foundation Trust hasn't changed much. Under Conservative leadership, the trust has 21 fewer beds than in 2010.

Unfortunatly, this isn't the case for many NHS trusts accross the country. Overall, there are 26003 fewer NHS beds in England than in 2010. That's a decrease of over 21%.

Over 50% of NHS trusts have fewer beds, whereas only around 10% of trusts have significantly more. See our summary page for the whole of England.
